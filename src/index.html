Testing for lead in Seattle Public Schools

Once every three years, Seattle Public Schools tests the drinking water at each campus for levels of lead above 10 parts per billion. The test results show, as of early 2018, at least one water source failed that standard at 41 schools and Memorial Stadium, where student athletes train and compete. A solution that Chicago Public Schools is piloting could potentially lower the levels of detectable lead to less than 2 parts per billion.
